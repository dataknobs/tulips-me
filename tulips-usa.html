Photography Tips

🌅
Go Early
Arrive at golden hour — fewer crowds and gorgeous warm light washing across the fields.
📷
Wide Angles Win
Use a wide-angle lens to capture the scale of the fields. Shoot low for dramatic foreground color.
👟
Come Prepared
Wear comfortable shoes — fields can be muddy. Bring a light jacket; spring mornings are cool.
📅
Check Bloom Updates
Timing varies by weather each year. Follow farm social accounts for real-time bloom reports.

Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Where are the best tulip fields in the U.S.?
Skagit Valley in Washington and Holland in Michigan consistently rank among the best, offering acres of vibrant blooms and rich visitor experiences. Oregon's Wooden Shoe Farm is another top contender.
When is tulip season in the United States?
Tulip season runs from February through May depending on location. Southern states like Texas bloom earliest (Feb–Mar), while northern destinations like Michigan and New York peak in late April and May.
Are tulip festivals worth visiting?
Absolutely. Beyond the stunning scenery, festivals offer live entertainment, local food, cultural events, and incredible photography opportunities. They're great for families, couples, and solo travelers.
Can you pick tulips at these festivals?
Some farms offer pick-your-own tulip experiences — Texas farms and Burnside Farms in Virginia are well known for this. Always check with individual farms in advance as availability varies by season.
