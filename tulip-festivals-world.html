7 Tulip Festivals You Must Visit

Spanning continents, cultures, and seasons — find your perfect bloom.

01
Most Famous · Netherlands

Keukenhof Gardens

The Garden of Europe

📍 Lisse, Netherlands 📅 March – May 🌸 7 million flowers

Keukenhof is the undisputed crown jewel of tulip destinations worldwide. Spread across 79 acres, it showcases over 7 million bulbs in meticulously designed garden settings — a once-in-a-lifetime spectacle that draws 1.5 million visitors every spring.

Endless sculpted tulip fields and canals
Stunning garden design and flower exhibitions
Over 800 varieties of tulips on display

Best for: First-time visitors & photographers

02
USA · Washington State

Skagit Valley Tulip Festival

America's Favorite Tulip Destination

📍 Washington State, USA 📅 April 🌸 Vast farmland fields

Set against the dramatic backdrop of the Cascade and Olympic mountains, Skagit Valley transforms every April into a painter's canvas. RoozenGaarde and Tulip Town farms draw photographers and families from across the continent.

Scenic mountain panorama backdrops
World-class local farms and gardens
Family-friendly activities and events

Best for: Photography & nature lovers

03
Turkey · Istanbul

Istanbul Tulip Festival

Where Tulips Were Born

📍 Istanbul, Turkey 📅 April 🌸 Millions across parks

Tulips originated in this very region, making Istanbul's festival deeply meaningful. Historic parks like Emirgan are blanketed with millions of blooms in geometric patterns, set against stunning Ottoman architecture and the Bosphorus.

Tulips in historic parks and gardens
Cultural performances and exhibitions
Unique designs and Ottoman-era backdrops

Best for: Culture & history lovers

04
Canada · Ottawa

Canadian Tulip Festival

A Festival With a Historic Story

📍 Ottawa, Canada 📅 May 🌸 1 million+ tulips

Born from a post-WWII gift of tulip bulbs from the Netherlands to Canada, this festival carries deep historical significance. Commissioner's Park along the Rideau Canal becomes a tapestry of over a million blooms each May.

Scenic park displays along Rideau Canal
Spectacular night light shows
Cultural events and food festivals

Best for: Families & history enthusiasts

05
United Kingdom

Spalding Tulip Festival

Traditional European Festival

📍 Lincolnshire, UK 📅 Spring (varies) 🌸 Classic floral floats

One of England's most beloved spring traditions, the Spalding festival features spectacular floral parade floats adorned with tulip heads, winding through the town's tulip-carpeted streets in a centuries-old celebration.

Iconic flower-covered parade floats
Historic Lincolnshire countryside setting
Traditional English spring festivities

Best for: European charm & tradition seekers

06
India · Kashmir

Srinagar Tulip Festival

Asia's Largest Tulip Garden

📍 Kashmir, India 📅 March – April 🌸 Thousands of varieties

Nestled in the foothills of the Himalayas and overlooking the tranquil Dal Lake, the Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden is a breathtaking sight — terraced hillsides cascading with hundreds of thousands of tulips in every hue.

Himalayan mountain backdrop
Sweeping terraced garden design
Extraordinary diversity of tulip varieties

Best for: Scenic views & adventurous travelers

07
Australia · Victoria

Tesselaar Tulip Festival

Southern Hemisphere's Finest Bloom

📍 Victoria, Australia 📅 September – October 🌸 Vibrant spring fields

For those in the Southern Hemisphere, Tesselaar in Victoria's Silvan Valley delivers spring tulip magic in September and October. Themed weekends, live entertainment, and food make it the region's most beloved floral event.

Themed weekends with entertainment
Vibrant fields of seasonal blooms
Family activities and local artisan markets

Best for: Southern Hemisphere spring travelers

Photography & Visit Tips
📅
Book Early
Popular festivals like Keukenhof and Skagit Valley sell out weeks in advance. Book tickets as soon as dates are announced.
🌅
Visit Early Morning
The best light and fewest crowds are in the first hour after opening. Dew on petals makes for magical photography.
📷
Use Natural Light
Shoot during golden hour. A wide angle captures field scale; get low for dramatic foreground color.
🌤️
Check Bloom Updates
Bloom timing shifts by 1–2 weeks depending on winter temperatures. Follow farm social accounts for real-time updates.
Common Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Where is the most famous tulip festival in the world?
Keukenhof Gardens in Lisse, Netherlands, is widely considered the most famous and spectacular tulip festival in the world, attracting over 1.5 million visitors each spring with 7+ million flowers across 79 acres.
When is tulip season around the world?
In the Northern Hemisphere, tulip season runs from March through May — earlier in southern regions like Texas and Turkey, later in Canada and the UK. In Australia, the Southern Hemisphere season peaks in September–October.
Are tulip festivals worth visiting?
Absolutely. They offer stunning natural beauty, cultural immersion, and unrivaled photography opportunities. Many festivals also include food, music, and local traditions that make them full travel experiences beyond just flowers.
Where can I see tulips in the United States?
The Skagit Valley Tulip Festival in Washington State is the premier U.S. destination, held each April. Other notable spots include Holland, Michigan, Woodburn, Oregon, and various farms in Texas that bloom as early as February.
