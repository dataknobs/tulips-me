Mystery, Power & the Extraordinary

Black tulips don't truly exist — like true blue, they are a near-miss that is more beautiful for the impossibility. Deep maroon-black varieties like Queen of Night are among the most dramatic flowers in any garden, carrying meanings of mystery, power, and the extraordinary.

Garden Planning

Beautiful Tulip Color Combinations

The best tulip displays use color strategically. These pairings are proven to work — visually, emotionally, and seasonally.

Red + White

The most classic contrast — pure white amplifies red's intensity. Formal, dramatic, timeless.

Purple + Yellow

Complementary colors at their finest — maximum visual impact, joyful and striking together.

Pink + White

Soft, romantic, and luminous — the most popular bridal pairing, beautiful in any setting.

Blue + Yellow

Complementary cool and warm — the most dramatic pairing for blue-violet varieties.

Red + Yellow + Purple

The tulip field palette — warm and cool together, the most energetic three-color combination.

All Pinks

Monochromatic blush through cerise — sophisticated, layered, and timelessly beautiful.

Orange + Yellow

Warm, joyful, and abundantly sunny — a combination that radiates pure spring energy.

Black + White

The most dramatic and theatrical pairing — Queen of Night with white creates a breathtaking contrast.
